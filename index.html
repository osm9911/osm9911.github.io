Sunmin Oh

About Me

I'm a Ph.D. student in Statistics at Seoul National University, advised by Prof. Gunwoong Park. I'm interested in topics related to directed acyclic graph (DAG) models, particularly in the area of structure learning algorithms. I'm also focused on developing theories and algorithms that relax traditional assumptions, such as identifiability conditions, faithfulness, and causal sufficiency, to enable learning of DAGs in a wider variety of data scenarios. Additionally, I am keen on research that integrates the DAG perspective with representation theory, causal AI, and explainable AI (XAI) to enhance the transparency and reliability of complex machine learning models.

Publications

Authors: Sunmin Oh, Seungsu Han, Gunwoong Park

Abstract: Much of science involves discovering and modeling causal relationships in nature. Significant progress has been made in developing statistical methods for representing and identifying causal knowledge from data using Linear Non-Gaussian Acyclic Models (LiNGAMs). Despite successes in learning LiNGAMs across various sample settings, the optimal sample complexity for high-dimensional LiNGAMs remains unexplored. This study establishes the optimal sample complexity for learning the structure of LiNGAMs under a sub-Gaussianity assumption. Specifically, it introduces a structure recovery algorithm using distance covariance that achieves the optimal sample complexity, without assuming faithfulness or a known indegree. The theoretical findings and superiority of the proposed algorithm compared to existing algorithms are validated through numerical experiments and real data analysis.
